Glossary

1. Color theory

Study of how colors mix, relate to each other and how they are visually perceived by people.

2. Color psychology

Field that analyzes how different tones and illuminations influence the behavior, emotions and decisions of the human being.

3. Semiology/Visual semiotics

Science that studies how visual elements and colors communicate meanings, symbols and concepts within a culture.

4. Chromatic stimulus

The physical and psychological reaction that is produced in the observer when perceiving the light of a determined color.

5. Emotional load

The set of feelings, memories or sensations that a color evokes in an automatic way in people.

6. Sociocultural meaning

The value, the interpretation or the status that a society or historical epoch assigns to a specific color (like purple for royalty).

7. Chromatic palette

Limited and ordered selection of colors that are chosen to maintain coherence in a design or visual project.

8. Chromatic selection

The strategic process of choosing color combinations based on a communication objective, beyond simple aesthetics.

9. Visual hierarchy by color

Use of contrast and saturation of colors to organize the elements of a design, guiding the attention of the spectator toward the most important thing.

10. Contrast

The difference of luminosity, tone or saturation between two or more colors placed one next to the other, which allows to distinguish them clearly.

11. Tonality/Tone

The quality that defines the color in itself within the visible spectrum (for example: red, green, blue).

12. Visual identity

The system of stable colors, shapes and graphics that build the recognizable personality of a brand or product.

13. Applied visual design

The practical and strategic use of visual elements to solve communication problems and organize information.

14. Indication of states

The use of specific color codes to communicate in an immediate way a situation (like green for success or red for danger).

15. Visual perception

The way in which the brain interprets light information and color received through the eyes.

16. Chromatic coherence

The maintenance and respect of the chosen color palette throughout an entire design to avoid visual chaos.

17. Pantone

Global system of identification and communication of color that assigns unique alphanumeric codes to ensure chromatic accuracy in any industry.

18. Color standardization

The process of unifying the criteria of colors through codes to avoid variations or errors in their production.

19. Technical precision of color

The guarantee that a color reproduces exactly with the same tone, saturation and brightness in different media or formats.

20. Chromatic equivalence

The exact translation of a color from one system to another (like passing from a physical guide to a digital profile) maintaining its visual fidelity.
