What did I learn at the conference?

1. Color theory

Studies the relationship between colors and their psychological impact on people. In web development, color transcends the aesthetic to become a tool that establishes the visual hierarchy of the interface and defines the brand identity of a site.
Each tone possesses a specific emotional load that the developer must understand to design digital experiences coherent with the purpose of the platform.

2. Color application in graphic proposals

Consists of the strategic implementation of chromatic palettes based on psychology, semiotics, and standardization systems. For the frontend, this translates into structuring style sheets (CSS) using well-defined color variables.
A correct application guarantees that the web interface is visually attractive, maintains brand coherence across all its pages, and intuitively guides user navigation.

3. Semiology of color

Analyzes the cultural meaning and the signs associated with each tone, explaining why red evokes urgency or blue transmits trust. In web development, understanding this context is crucial, since meanings vary according to the target audience.
Applying semiology allows selecting the appropriate colors according to the niche of the application, ensuring that the visual message is interpreted correctly by the users.

4. Use of pantone

Pantone provides unique global codes to guarantee color precision in different industries. Although it belongs to the physical world, in web development its utility lies in the exact translation of brand identity to digital environments through equivalences in HEX or RGB values.
This ensures that screen colors faithfully match the original design and the physical products of the company.

5. Applied visual design

Focuses on the use of color as a functional element that guides the user and indicates the state of the system. In the web environment, this determines the creation of clear interactive components: a green button for successful actions, gray text for secondary elements, and red alerts for critical errors.
Its strategic use directly improves usability (UX) and visually organizes the information of the page.
