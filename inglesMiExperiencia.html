My personal experience

What I learned

I understood the impact of color psychology and how different shades influence human perception and emotions. Technically, each color acts as a visual stimulus capable of evoking specific contexts —such as the association of red with heat or urgency—, which allows designing interfaces that connect emotionally with the user.

Lived experiences

The day represented a significant experience of discovery that was enriched by debating ideas with my classmates in an informal way after the event. This exchange of perspectives made it possible to consolidate the acquired knowledge about design and usability, making the learning process dynamic and integrating from beginning to end.

Aspects that caught your attention

I found the historical analysis of the sociocultural value of color extremely interesting. Emblematic cases like the use of purple as a symbol of status and royalty due to its complex ancient manufacturing, or the myths about the toxicity of green surrounding the death of Napoleon, demonstrate that color possesses a dense cultural background that impacts public perception.

Relationship between creative industry and software development.

There is a direct and strategic relationship between creative disciplines and software development. The integration of visual design in technology allows structuring much clearer, accessible, and easy-to-interpret user interfaces (UI), transforming technical complexity into an intuitive digital experience for any user profile.

Personal reflection on how these spaces strengthen your professional training.

This learning strengthens my technical training in a transversal way. Although at first glance color theory seems foreign to pure code, mastering the visual influence on human behavior provides me with a key competence to collaborate in multidisciplinary projects and develop user-oriented technological solutions.

Application of color in user experience.

The strategic application of color is fundamental to optimize the user experience (UX) and brand consistency. By employing a coherent chromatic palette, we not only make it easier for the user to recognize the identity of the product, but we also create intuitive interfaces that reduce the cognitive load and guide navigation naturally.

Importance of Pantone and visual semiology.

Visual semiology defines the cultural meaning of chromatic signs, while the Pantone system standardizes their digital and physical production. Together, they guarantee that the color attributes chosen for an interface communicate the correct message and are deployed with absolute technical precision on any screen or device on the market.
