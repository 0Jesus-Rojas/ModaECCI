My final reflection

Before attending the event, I considered that the fashion industry had no relation to my career, since my focus was centered on the backend: code, algorithms and databases. However, ECCI Fashion demonstrated to me that design is a transversal competence that directly impacts the user experience (UX) of any product.
Sara Viloria's conference on color theory was key to changing my perspective. I understood that chromatic selection is not just aesthetics, but a fundamental strategic decision for front-end development. Now I understand that when choosing the color of a button I am communicating an action; when adjusting the contrast of a text I guarantee accessibility and when defining a palette for a project I build the visual identity of the interface.
The event also made me reflect on the psychology of color and its capacity to communicate deep meanings and emotions. During an activity where we had to associate a color with an emotion, I chose gray as a representation of tranquility. For me, this tone evokes the serenity of the gray mornings of childhood during family trips, a feeling of calm to which it is always pleasant to return.
